Paramatachia tubicola (Hickman, 1950)

The most splendid little spider with a 'Marvel' worthy origin story and a talent often neglected of mention.

The word "paramatachia" is from a dead hybrid language: Basque/ American-Indian- 'Micmac Pidgin'. This is a taxonomial novelty in a field dominated by Greek and (often 'vulgar' and misused) Latin.

This language was developed through the fur trade between the French and American Indians in the 1500 and 1600s.

The 'Matachia' in 'Paramatachia' refered to adornment. The journal of a French fur trader - Jean-Baptist Truteau (1794-96) says: "Mat(t)acher derives from the noun matachia (also spelled matachias, matachiat, mattachiat),which seems to be of Basque origin.... From a Basque-Micmac pidgin spoken in the Gulf of St Lawrence and on the Atlantic, the word came into the French spoken in New France, at first as a noun referring loosely to ornaments traded with or made by Indians, such as beads, necklaces, bracelets, belts, embroidered accessories, and also to ornamentations of colored porcupine quills."
